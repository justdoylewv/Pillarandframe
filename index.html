The old way is breaking

The reliable way to grow a B2B company is stopping. You can feel it.

  • LinkedIn ad costs are climbing every quarter. The CPM math gets worse, not better.
  • AI is commoditizing content faster than any agency can outpace. A "great" post is now a Tuesday afternoon for anyone with a prompt.
  • Senior buyers ignore everything that smells like marketing. They read what their peers send them.
  • Cold outreach is dying. Every senior person you want to reach has 200 unanswered DMs.
  • Your competitors are louder online. You do better work. The buyer can't tell.
  • "Build authority" sounds nice. No one tells you exactly how.

The solution

Introducing The Authority Engine.

The Authority Engine does the opposite of buying attention. It accumulates it. The mechanism is a network effect. You stop chasing prospects through cold outreach and ads. Instead, you invite the exact people you want as clients to be on a podcast-style series you host. The interview itself is the beginning of the relationship. Everything else is downstream.

Q&A.

Questions we hear every time.

Why LinkedIn only? Aren't we losing reach on Instagram, YouTube, X?

Algorithmic momentum on one channel beats noise across five. When you start fresh on a channel, distribution is rationed until the algorithm learns who you are. The fastest way to teach it is consistency in one place. Once you have real momentum (usually around month six) we open a second channel. Most B2B founders we work with end up on LinkedIn primary, with YouTube as the long-form home for the episode. That covers the buyer.

Why does this have to be founder-led? Couldn't it be cheaper if you delegated production?

The founder-led part is the product. Selection of who is worth featuring, framing of the conversation, direction of the production, the read on a guest's positioning during the debrief. Those are all judgment calls. They are the scarce inputs. They are exactly the inputs AI and commodity content shops cannot supply. Delegating them collapses the work back into something a competitor with a bigger budget could match in a week. Founder-led is what makes this defensible.

What if we can't book a guest for a given month?

The five-step outreach sequence is built so you have 3 to 5 guests in flight at any time. If month four's primary cancels, month five's secondary moves up. By month three the guest list starts feeding itself: prior guests refer the next ones. If a month genuinely slips, we use the slot for a founder-led solo episode instead, captured in the same format. You never go dark.

Do we own the content?

Yes. Everything. Work-for-hire. Your Voice Profile, your Quote Library, every asset we produce. We retain working copies for production use only. If the engagement ends, you keep operating from the same materials.

Who do we have to be for this to work?

You are likely a fit if: you run a $5M to $50M B2B company, you are growth-stage past product-market fit, you sell to enterprise or mid-market buyers, your sales cycle is months not days, you are willing to be on camera, and you believe the next decade rewards depth over volume.

You are likely not a fit if: you sell to consumers, you sell short-cycle transactional products, you will not be on camera, you want a faster funnel or a cheaper ad, or you are looking for high-volume content at the lowest cost per asset.

What's the time commitment from me?

After onboarding: about 3 hours a month. 60 to 90 minutes for the interview. 60 minutes for the monthly coaching call. 30 minutes reviewing assets. Your team's operator carries the outreach motion (3 to 5 hours a week) under our coaching.

What does "the debrief" actually mean?

After each interview, your guest is offered a 30 to 45 minute follow-up. Framed as a thank-you. The call is a genuine strategic read on the guest's positioning, delivered by you, with Pillar & Frame's prep work behind it. No pitch. A meaningful share of guests leave that conversation wanting to work with the company that just spent 45 minutes giving them a real read on their position. That is how the engine converts.

What if it doesn't work?

There is no version of running this for 12 months where you end without a Voice Profile, a Quote Library, a relationship network, and an owned audience. Those exist regardless of whether sales also closed. The 3-month minimum gives us enough runway to publish month two and month three's full asset sets, so you can decide based on what you see, not on what we promised.
